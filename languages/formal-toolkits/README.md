## üìö Complete Curriculum

### 1. Mathematics

#### Stage 1: Foundational Math (Rules of Computation)
**Duration**: 4-6 weeks
**Textbook**: *Precalculus* by Stewart, Redlin, and Watson
**Topics**: Algebra, functions, trigonometry
**Goal**: Computational fluency for all other disciplines

#### Stage 2: Calculus & Applied Math (Science of Systems)
**Duration**: 8-12 weeks
**Textbooks**:
- *Calculus: Early Transcendentals* by James Stewart
- *Introduction to Linear Algebra* by Gilbert Strang

**Topics**:
- Single and multivariable calculus
- Linear algebra (vectors, matrices, transformations)
- Differential equations basics

**Unlocks**: Economics, Physics, Data Science, Cryptography

#### Stage 3: Pure & Abstract Math (Art of Structure)
**Duration**: 12-16 weeks
**Textbooks**:
- *How to Prove It* by Daniel J. Velleman
- *Understanding Analysis* by Stephen Abbott

**Topics**:
- Mathematical proofs
- Real analysis
- Abstract thinking

#### Stage 4: Advanced Topics
**Textbooks**:
- *Abstract Algebra* by Dummit and Foote
- *Real and Complex Analysis* by Walter Rudin

**Topics**: Advanced pure mathematics (PhD level)

---

### 2. Logic

#### Stage 1: Informal Logic (Art of Argument)
**Duration**: 2-3 weeks
**Textbooks**:
- *An Illustrated Book of Bad Arguments* by Ali Almossawi
- *A Rulebook for Arguments* by Anthony Weston

**Topics**: Logical fallacies, argumentation basics
**Critical for**: All analytical thinking, spotting bad reasoning

#### Stage 2: Propositional Logic (Science 101)
**Duration**: 4-6 weeks
**Textbook**: *A Concise Introduction to Logic* by Patrick J. Hurley

**Topics**:
- Symbolic logic (and, or, if...then, not)
- Truth tables
- Validity vs soundness

**Critical for**: Computer Science, Intelligence Analysis, Law

#### Stage 3: First-Order Predicate Logic (Workhorse Model)
**Duration**: 6-8 weeks
**Textbook**: *The Logic Book* by Bergmann, Moor, and Nelson

**Topics**:
- Quantifiers (for all, there exists)
- Natural deduction
- Formal proof systems

#### Stage 4: Metalogic & Computability (PhD Level)
**Duration**: 8-12 weeks
**Textbook**: *Computability and Logic* by Boolos, Burgess, and Jeffrey

**Topics**: G√∂del's theorems, computability theory

---

### 3. Statistics

#### Stage 1: Descriptive Statistics & Skepticism
**Duration**: 3-4 weeks
**Textbook**: *How to Lie with Statistics* by Darrell Huff

**Topics**:
- Correlation ‚â† causation
- Selection bias
- Data visualization tricks
- Statistical skepticism

**Critical for**: Intelligence Analysis, avoiding disinformation

#### Stage 2: Inferential Statistics (Science of Inference)
**Duration**: 8-10 weeks
**Textbook**: *Statistics* by Freedman, Pisani, and Purves

**Topics**:
- Descriptive statistics (mean, median, standard deviation)
- Inferential statistics (hypothesis testing, p-values, confidence intervals)
- Regression analysis
- Experimental design

**Critical for**: All data-driven disciplines

**Alternative**: *The Art of Statistics* by David Spiegelhalter (more accessible)

#### Stage 3: Machine Learning & Data Science
**Duration**: 10-12 weeks
**Textbooks**:
- *An Introduction to Statistical Learning* (ISLR) by James et al.
- *The Elements of Statistical Learning* (ESL) by Hastie et al. (more advanced)

**Topics**:
- Supervised learning (classification, regression)
- Unsupervised learning (clustering, dimensionality reduction)
- Model selection and validation
- Feature engineering

**Critical for**: Cybersecurity (anomaly detection), Intelligence Analysis

#### Stage 4: Bayesian & Causal Inference
**Duration**: 8-10 weeks
**Textbook**: *The Book of Why* by Judea Pearl

**Topics**:
- Bayesian statistics (updating beliefs)
- Causal inference (proving causation)
- Directed Acyclic Graphs (DAGs)
- Counterfactual reasoning

**Critical for**: Intelligence Analysis (ultimate goal)

---

### 4. Computer Science

#### Stage 1: Computational Thinking (Art of Computation)
**Duration**: 2-3 weeks
**Textbook**: *CODE* by Charles Petzold

**Topics**: How computers work fundamentally
**Goal**: Intuitive understanding before coding

#### Stage 2: Programming Fundamentals (Science 101)
**Duration**: 6-8 weeks
**Textbook**: *Automate the Boring Stuff with Python* by Al Sweigart

**Topics**:
- Python basics
- Variables, loops, functions
- File I/O, web scraping
- Automation

**Critical for**: Cybersecurity (absolutely essential)

#### Stage 3: Algorithms & Data Structures (Science of Algorithms)
**Duration**: 12-16 weeks
**Textbook**: *Introduction to Algorithms* (CLRS) by Cormen et al.

**Topics**:
- Data structures (arrays, trees, graphs, hash tables)
- Algorithms (sorting, searching, graph algorithms)
- Complexity analysis (Big O)
- Dynamic programming

**Critical for**: Advanced Cybersecurity, technical interviews

#### Stage 4: System Design (Professional Art)
**Duration**: 8-12 weeks
**Textbook**: *Designing Data-Intensive Applications* by Martin Kleppmann

**Topics**:
- Scalable system architecture
- Distributed systems
- Databases
- Reliability and maintainability

## üéØ Parallel Learning Track (Months 1-6)

### Month 1: Foundations Blitz

**Logic** (30min/week):
- Week 1-2: *An Illustrated Book of Bad Arguments*
- Week 3-4: *A Rulebook for Arguments*

**Mathematics** (30min/week):
- Review algebra basics
- Start *Precalculus* (chapters 1-3)

**Computer Science** (1h/week):
- Week 1-2: *CODE* (complete)
- Week 3-4: Start *Automate the Boring Stuff* (ch 1-3)

**Statistics** (15min/week):
- Begin *How to Lie with Statistics*

**Total**: 2.25h/week

---

### Month 2-3: Building Momentum

**Logic** (30min/week):
- Start *A Concise Introduction to Logic*
- Chapters 1-4 (informal logic, categorical logic)

**Mathematics** (45min/week):
- Continue *Precalculus*
- Focus on functions, trigonometry

**Computer Science** (1h/week):
- *Automate the Boring Stuff* chapters 4-11
- Build small automation scripts

**Statistics** (30min/week):
- Complete *How to Lie with Statistics*
- Start *Statistics* by Freedman

**Total**: 2.75h/week (slightly above 15% - acceptable)

---

### Month 4-6: Intermediate Push

**Logic** (30min/week):
- Complete *Concise Introduction to Logic* Stage 2
- Start first-order logic (Stage 3)

**Mathematics** (45min/week):
- Complete *Precalculus*
- Begin *Calculus* (derivatives, integrals)
- Start *Linear Algebra* basics

**Computer Science** (1h/week):
- Complete *Automate the Boring Stuff*
- Begin *Introduction to Algorithms*
- Contribute to open-source or build security tools

**Statistics** (30min/week):
- *Statistics* by Freedman (chapters on inference)
- Hypothesis testing, confidence intervals

**Total**: 2.75h/week
